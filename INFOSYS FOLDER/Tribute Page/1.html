Father of nation

Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, also known as the Father of the Nation, was born on 2 October 1869 in Porbandar, Gujarat. His full name was Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi. He led India's freedom struggle against British rule through the principles of truth (Satya) and non-violence (Ahimsa). Gandhi inspired millions of people to join peaceful movements such as the Non-Cooperation Movement, the Salt March (Dandi March), and the Quit India Movement. His philosophy of non-violent resistance influenced leaders around the world. He was assassinated on 30 January 1948 in New Delhi. His life continues to inspire people to practice peace, honesty, and justice.

Mahatma Gandhi played a vital role in India's struggle for freedom from British rule. He believed in truth (Satya) and non-violence (Ahimsa) and encouraged people to fight peacefully. His major contributions include: Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-22): Urged Indians to boycott British schools, courts, and goods. Salt March (Dandi March, 1930): Led a 240-mile march to protest the British salt tax. Civil Disobedience Movement: Encouraged people to peacefully disobey unfair British laws. Quit India Movement (1942): Called on the British to "Quit India," inspiring nationwide protests. Promotion of Unity: Worked to unite people of different religions and communities and promoted equality.

What Inspires Me Most

When Mahatma Gandhi was a young boy, he once made a mistake and felt very guilty. Instead of hiding the truth, he wrote a letter to his father confessing everything and asking for forgiveness. His father did not punish him. Instead, he silently forgave him with tears in his eyes. This moment deeply touched Gandhi and taught him that truth and honesty are more powerful than fear and lies. As he grew up, Gandhi followed these values throughout his life. He led India's freedom struggle through truth (Satya) and non-violence (Ahimsa), proving that courage does not require violence. His peaceful leadership inspired millions of Indians and people around the world. Moral: Always be truthful, admit your mistakes, and face challenges with courage and honesty. Even a single honest act can change your life and inspire others.
